Trend Interpretation Override

1. ADPIE — Assessment first

Assess unless immediate danger demands action first.

2. Maslow

Physiological → safety → psychosocial → teaching.

3. Risk Reduction

Treat the condition that can deteriorate quickest.

4. Least Invasive / Least Restrictive

Simple → noninvasive → invasive.

5. Expected vs Unexpected

Unexpected or worsening findings need intervention.
Expected needs monitoring/teaching.

6. Delegation Rules

RN: unstable, initial assessments, teaching, IV pushes, evaluation.

LPN: stable, predictable outcomes, reinforce teaching, oral/IM meds.

UAP: ADLs, hygiene, vitals, I&O, positioning.
When unsure → RN keeps unstable or changing client.

ADVANCED ADD-ON MODULES
A. Med Surg II – Advanced Priorities

Use these high-level rules:

Cardiac

Chest pain = immediate assessment + ECG

Nitro requires BP check

Digoxin toxicity: vision changes, nausea, low HR

Hypokalemia increases digoxin toxicity risk

New dysrhythmia = unstable

Pulmonary edema → sit up + oxygen

Renal/Fluid

AKI: rising BUN/Cr, low urine output

Hyperkalemia = priority (can cause fatal dysrhythmias)

Fluid overload: crackles, JVD, edema, weight gain

Nephrotoxic drugs (aminoglycosides, contrast dye)

Neuro

ANY change in LOC = priority

Stroke: time is brain, assess glucose, last known well

Increased ICP: headache, vomiting, decreased LOC, bradycardia, hypertension

Respiratory

Stridor = airway emergency

New oxygen saturation drop = immediate intervention

Post-op atelectasis: encourage IS, ambulation, deep breathing

C. OB Expert Logic

Late decelerations → turn mother left, stop oxytocin, oxygen, IV fluids

Variable decels → reposition

Fetal bradycardia = emergency

Postpartum hemorrhage → massage fundus

Magnesium toxicity → low RR, low reflexes, treat with calcium gluconate
